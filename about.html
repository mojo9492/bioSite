About Patrick

Patrick Star is a man of many talents. He is a Ranger CBRN Sergeant in 75th Ranger Regiment and is tasked as a software developer for a Ranger Innovation cell whose goal is to solve Ranger problems with Ranger software. In 2006, he was a paramedic/firefighter for the Savannah Fire Department up until he joined the Army in 2011. He is a lifelong learner who only wants to be the best version of himself; he has the drive to succeed. This is clear as Patrick has a bachelor's degree in biology and is enrolled in City University of Seattle to obtain his master's degree in computer science. Patrick spends his time running, working out, and hiking and always strives to stay active. When he isn't hitting the gym or writing code, he can be found reading. His favorite book is *Dune* by Frank Herbert, and his favorite author is the critically acclaimed Erik Larson, who brought us nonfictional pieces such as *The Devil in White City* and *Thunderstruck*. He also is a consumer of fine role-playing games like Dungeons and Dragons, a fantasy RPG, or Vaesen, which is a gothic style, Nordic mythology themed RPG. He is a natural tinkerer, who is diving into Arduino micro controllers and open-source intelligence OSINT projects, which aims to expand the network and transparency of news reporting and intelligence gathering. Patrick Star is a man of the modern renaissance and will continue to have a positive impact to the world and those around him.

Interview

Welcome Mr. Star and thanks for taking the time to do this interview. Let's start off by stating for the record who you are and what you do.

"I am Patrick Star and I work in the Army as a software developer."

What are your favorite pass times?

"Well, I enjoy reading everything, country and ballroom dancing, and hiking. I am a general health nut (including fasting, meditation, and a lot of physical activity). I am looking at getting back into competitive Olympic lifting. I enjoy role playing games like D&D as well as Vaesen. I'm also taking college courses whether I need them or not, and I just started getting really into Arduino projects and learning OSINT techniques. I have also been an avid journal keep for years and am starting to get into gardening. Oh, and I still keep up to date with emergency medicine including current paramedic practices (partially to keep my certs up) as well as hazmat medicine and rescue techniques."

So you are attending college, right now. What is your area of study?

"I currently have a BS of Biology, but I am working on my capstone for my Master in Computer Science"

Why did you choose to make the switch from biology to computer science?

"I was in biology because I planned on going to med school but working insane hours on no sleep did not enable a high enough GPA for that to be possible. I switched fields to computer science because I started getting interested in tech towards the end of undergrad and had a lot of tech buddies here."

Mr. Star, I notice you have an affinity for computers, when did this happen and what clicked for you to start programming?

"College, when I realized that I was the only one in my friend group that didn't know shit about computers or graphing calculators"

I will mark that down as peer pressure, which is very powerful stuff. You told me that you were a paramedic before the Army? Tell me more about a normal day as a paramedic in GA.

"You are either working 8 hour shifts taking old people to and from nursing homes or working 911 calls which are 24 on 48 off shifts. Mostly old people and heart attacks but downtown gets a fair bit of shootings and other trauma. Structure fires were rare but did almost have a roof come down on me. Mostly it was work till the coffee couldn't keep me awake and somewhere in the middle take a test at college", "Always short handed so we worked overtime whether we wanted to or not."

Sounds like it was offered an unparalleled experience. Let's talk about your goals. Where do you see yourself in 5 years?

"I see myself still in the army in 5 years but paid more. Hopefully by then I'll have a second masters degree (in cyber security) and will be at least starting a PhD"

You must be in it for the long haul. Has there been a favorite place you frequently hike in WA?

"Capital forest is where I have gone the most (other than the trails by my house) but sometimes I go into the north cascades where I either hike up to an old mining town or to diablo lake. Rainier is also good."

Reading is something we all need to do more of. What are you currently reading? What is your favorite book or who is your favorite author?

"Everything Terry Pratchett ever wrote, love Discworld, Pebbles of Perception by Endersen, Unshakable by Tony Robbins, and Mentatsof Dune by Brian Herbert. Hard to answer that but I'd say that Frank Herbert's Dune is my favorite followed by Erik Larson's books. He had a book No One Goes Alone recently that I really like and have read most of his other books."

What OSINT projects are currently working on?

"Right now I am practicing with geolocation games using a combination of reverse image searching and web scraping using AI."

Do you have any advice for someone trying to follow in your footsteps?

"1 - recruiters lie 2 - be curious and try a wide range of activities till you find stuff that you lose track of time doing 3 - invest early in fire/forget funds because compound interest is how you exploit the game of life 4 - journal 5 - find the key tasks that you can do every day that lead you in the general direction that you want to go (for that read "Atomic habits", "The Power of Habit", and "The One Thing")"
